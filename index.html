How the forecast is built
Many sources, blended into one honest read.

Slabcast doesn't guess. Every break runs through the same model: swell fit, wind, and tide. We feed that model as many real, free, public sources as we can pull. More inputs, more confidence. Here's everything mixing into today's read:

Open-Meteo Marine
Global wave models (GWAM + a best-match swell ensemble), swell height, period and direction, hour by hour.
NOAA NDBC buoys
Real-time offshore buoy readings. The near-term forecast is bias-corrected toward what the buoys are actually measuring.
CDIP buoys (Scripps)
Coastal Data Information Program nearshore wave buoys, a second live check on size and period close to shore.
NOAA CO-OPS tides
Exact hourly tide predictions per region, so each break is scored at its preferred tide stage.
Open-Meteo wind
Hourly wind speed and direction at every break, offshore, glassy, cross or onshore.

Why Slabcast exists

Surfing is free. Anyone can paddle out. Slabcast runs on the same idea: an honest read on what's coming, for every surfer, with nothing in the way. No ads. No pop ups. No logins. No subscription. No data sold.

And no spin. We don't hype a flat day into a swell, and we don't talk down a good one. The score is the score. Check it against the buoys and your own eyes. Wind moves most inside 48 hours.
